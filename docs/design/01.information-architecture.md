# GUARDIANS public web presence — information architecture

### This document's audience

Content strategists, project managers, web designers and developers.

### This document's purpose

Define how the website’s content, navigation, and page structure will be organised so users can find what they need and the site team can build it coherently.

---

## Site purpose

Build a public web presence for GUARDIANS that explains the program, helps users discover services and resources, and represents partner contributions clearly and equitably.

The site should support two linked modes:

1. **Program communication** — what GUARDIANS is, why it exists, who coordinates, funds and delivers it.
2. **Resource discovery** — what services, tools, guidance, infrastructure, code, training and pilots have been produced or are in development.

---

## Site primary audiences

| Audience                   | Main question                                   | Primary destination                     |
| -------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Researcher users           | What can I use or learn from?                   | Services & resources                    |
| Existing partners          | Is our work represented accurately?             | Partners & contributions                |
| Potential partners         | Where could we contribute or collaborate?       | Home, Partners & contributions, Contact |
| Peer programs / consortia  | What model, outputs or components are reusable? | Services & resources, About             |
| Funders, government, media | What is GUARDIANS and is it delivering?         | Home, About                             |

---

## Global navigation

A simple top-level navigation structure:

```text
Home
Services & resources
Partners & contributions
About
Contact
```

Primary CTA across the site:

> Explore services & resources

Secondary CTA:

> Contact the GUARDIANS team

## Global footer

A simple site-wide footer should appear on all pages.

Required footer elements:

1. Short program description or contact route.
2. Site-wide funding acknowledgement.
3. Bioplatforms Australia and NCRIS attribution, with logos where approved assets are available.

Build notes:

- Keep funder attribution visually distinct from partner contribution content.
- Use restrained footer styling; this is acknowledgement, not a second navigation model.
- Use approved wording and logo usage when available.

# Site map and page requirements

## 1. `/` — Home

### Page purpose

Orient visitors quickly, establish credibility, and direct them to the most relevant next step.

### Key user questions

- What is GUARDIANS?
- Why does it matter?
- What is being built?
- Who is involved?
- Where should I go next?

### Required sections

1. **Hero**
   - One-sentence definition.
   - Short supporting paragraph.
   - Primary CTA: **Explore services & resources**
   - Secondary CTA: **See partner contributions**

   Definition (draft):

   > GUARDIANS is a national partnership building secure, interoperable infrastructure for discovering, responsibly accessing, and analysing sensitive human omics data.

2. **Why GUARDIANS exists**
   - Plain-language problem statement.
   - Focus on fragmentation, sensitivity of human omics data, access barriers, governance complexity and the need for trusted national infrastructure.

3. **What GUARDIANS is building**
   - Capability cards linking to catalogue filters or grouped catalogue sections.
   - Use the controlled [capability](#capability-areas) vocabulary

4. **Delivered with partners**
   - Short explanation of the delivery model.
   - Partner logo/list strip.
   - Link to `/partners-contributions`.
   - Keep this as a credibility and orientation section; do not duplicate the full partner contribution index.

5. **Recent activity and outputs**
   - Short list of recent outputs, updates, releases, events, publications or repositories.
   - Keep this lightweight and remove entries when their underlying catalogue item or destination is removed.

6. **Contact / next step**
   - Short CTA block linking to `/contact`.

### Build notes

- Home should act as a routing page, not a complete explanation of everything.
- Include a link to `/about` from either **Why GUARDIANS exists** or **Delivered with partners**.
- Reuse the canonical one-sentence description consistently.

## 2. `/services-resources` — Services & resources catalogue

### Page purpose

Help users discover GUARDIANS outputs by capability, type, status, visibility/access and project.

This is the main utility page.

### Key user questions

- What has GUARDIANS produced?
- What can I use now?
- What is in development?
- Which capability area does this relate to?
- Who led or contributed to it?
- Where do I go next?

### Required sections

1. **Catalogue introduction**
   - Explain that the catalogue includes available services as well as enabling resources, pilots, infrastructure components, guidance, code, training and publications.
   - Avoid implying that every item is a public-facing service.

2. **Filters**
   - Capability area
   - Output type
   - Status
   - Visibility / access
   - Project filter support via URL parameters for links originating from `/partners-contributions`
   - Include opinionated popular pathways above the full filter set for common user tasks.
   - Support multi-select filters for detailed refinement.
   - Use grouped checkbox lists for expanded filters, especially where values are long or numerous.

3. **Catalogue cards**

   | Field                                            |          Required | Notes                                                                        |
   | ------------------------------------------------ | ----------------: | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
   | Name                                             |               Yes | Clear title                                                                  |
   | Short description                                |               Yes | One or two sentences                                                         |
   | User value                                       |               Yes | What someone can do with it                                                  |
   | [Capability areas](#capability-areas)            |               Yes | One or more capability values (controlled vocab)                             |
   | [Output type](#output-types)                     |               Yes | What is the thing a user can find, request, use, refer to (controlled vocab) |
   | [Status](#status-values)                         |               Yes | Maturity/readiness (controlled vocab)                                        |
   | [Visibility / access](#visibility-access-values) |               Yes | Who can see/use it (controlled vocab)                                        |
   | Lead organisation(s)                             |               Yes | Primary attribution; may include co-leads                                    |
   | Contributing organisation(s)                     |          Optional | Shared attribution                                                            |
   | [Contribution type](#contribution-types)         |               Yes | What kind of work was contributed (controlled vocab)                         |
   | Partner contribution slugs                       |               Yes | Explicit links to one or more project contribution entries                   |
   | Link                                             | Yes, if available | External resource, repository, publication, service page or contact pathway  |
   | Intended users                                   |          Optional | Researchers, data custodians, infrastructure providers, partners             |

4. **Catalogue CTA**
   - Primary: use the service or resource via the link
   - Secondary: "Can’t find what you need? Contact the GUARDIANS team."

### Build notes

- Catalogue cards may expose supporting metadata in a collapsed details block rather than always displaying full attribution inline.
- Status, visibility/access and output-type labels should remain visible, but may sit in the bottom metadata row alongside the details disclosure rather than competing with the card title.
- Catalogue items should carry explicit project links through controlled partner-contribution slug values rather than relying only on organisation overlap.
- If an item has no public destination, link to a contact pathway or partner contribution entry rather than leaving it feeling unfinished.
- Do not treat "internal / enabling" as a status. It is a visibility/access classification.

## 3. `/partners-contributions` — Project contribution index

### Page purpose

Represent partner work fairly while preserving user-oriented navigation elsewhere.

This page should show how project-level contributions map to GUARDIANS capabilities and outputs. It should not become a set of institutional marketing pages.

### Key user questions

- Who is involved in GUARDIANS?
- What project-level contributions are being delivered?
- Which lead partner organisation or organisations are associated with each project?
- How does partner delivery fit into the overall program?

### Required sections

1. **Partner delivery model**
   - Explain that GUARDIANS is coordinated by Australian BioCommons and delivered with national partners.
   - Clarify that contributions include public-facing services and enabling work.

2. **Project contribution index**

   Each project entry should include:

   | Field                                      | Required | Notes                                                       |
   | ------------------------------------------ | -------: | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
   | Project slug                               |      Yes | Controlled value used for explicit catalogue/project linkage |
   | Project title                              |      Yes | Use supplied proposal project title                         |
   | Lead partner organisation(s)               |      Yes | Represented with organisation logos                         |
   | Short project description                  |      Yes | Based on implementation-plan purpose                        |
   | [Contribution areas](#capability-areas)    |      Yes | Mapped to capability areas                                  |
   | Related services/resources                 | Optional | Linked via filtered catalogue views                         |

3. **Cross-links**
   - Capability chips on project entries should link to filtered catalogue views using both capability and project slug where there is a clear relationship.
   - Catalogue items may link back to project contribution entries where useful.

### Build notes

- Avoid long partner narrative profiles.
- Use structured project entries so representation is consistent and maintainable.
- Keep logos at organisation level while presenting contributions at project level.
- Keep controlled contribution types available in the IA and data model, but do not render a standalone vocabulary section on this page.
- Do not make the site primarily partner-first; this page exists to support attribution and findability.

### Page purpose

Provide authoritative context for the program without overloading the homepage.

### Key user questions

- What is GUARDIANS?
- Who coordinates it?
- How is it funded?
- What phase is it in?
- How does it relate to national digital research infrastructure?

### Required sections

1. **Program overview**
   - What GUARDIANS is.
   - What problem it addresses.
   - Who it is intended to support.

2. **Coordination and delivery**
   - Australian BioCommons coordination role.
   - Partner delivery model.
   - Relationship to the broader national infrastructure ecosystem.

3. **Program timeline**
   - Four-year program.
   - Delivery started in late 2024.
   - Current funded delivery window runs to Q3 2028.
   - Link to the live milestone tracker where useful.

4. **Funding acknowledgement**
   - Recognise BPA and NCRIS funding/support as appropriate.
   - Use approved wording when available.

5. **Related links**
   - BioCommons GUARDIANS page.
   - Program announcements.
   - Relevant partner or funder pages.
   - Selected list of publications, events.

8. **Next step**
   - Link to `/services-resources` as the concrete expression of program delivery.

### Build notes

- This page should be credible and factual, not promotional.
- Avoid duplicating the homepage. Home orients; About explains.
- About should not be a dead end: link users back to the catalogue and contact pathways.

## 5. `/contact` — Contact

### Page purpose

Give different audiences a clear next step without requiring them to understand the program structure first.

### Key user questions

- Who do I contact?
- How do I ask about using something?
- How do I discuss collaboration?
- How do I provide feedback?

### Required sections

1. **Contact intro**
   - Short statement inviting enquiries.

2. **Audience-based pathways**

   Use 3–4 contact cards:
   - **Using services and resources**
     - For researchers, data custodians or infrastructure operators.
     - Secondary link: `/services-resources`

   - **Discuss collaboration**
     - For potential partners or peer programs.
     - Secondary link: `/partners-contributions`

   - **Communications and operations**
     - For communications, reporting or program-level questions.
     - Secondary link: `/about`

   - **Give feedback on this site**
     - For site feedback, content corrections and findability suggestions.

3. **Response expectation**
   - Optional short line about the team reviewing enquiries.

### Build notes

- Contact should not be the only next step. Where possible, help users self-serve by linking back to relevant pages.
- Keep contact pathways audience-based, not organisationally structured.

# Cross-linking rules

- All pages are available through global navigation.
- Home capability cards link to relevant catalogue filters or grouped sections.
- Home featured services/resources link to catalogue items, filtered views or grouped sections.
- Home partner section links to `/partners-contributions`.
- Home program-background links point to `/about`.
- Catalogue cards show lead and contributing organisations as attribution.
- Partner entries link to filtered catalogue views using explicit project linkage and capability filters.
- About links to `/services-resources` as the concrete expression of program delivery.
- Contact cards link back to relevant self-service pages where useful.
- External links should not replace internal structure; use them as next-step destinations once the user understands the GUARDIANS context.

# Cross-site content rules

## Language

Use plain, direct language.

**Specific guidance**

- Use "omics" as the umbrella term. Use "genomics" only where referring specifically to genomics.
- Use "services and resources" rather than "services" only (not all outputs are public-facing services).

## Controlled vocabularies

Use the following **capability areas**<a id="capability-areas"></a> consistently across the site:

- Data discovery
- Data commons and repositories
- Scalable analysis environments
- Trusted researcher identity
- Data access and authorisation
- Governance, policy and operations

These website capability labels should be treated as the controlled values used in content. They map approximately to the roadmap-facing capability framing as follows:

| Website capability | Roadmap-facing framing |
| --- | --- |
| Data discovery | Discover data & services |
| Data commons and repositories | Host & steward data |
| Scalable analysis environments | Analyse data |
| Trusted researcher identity | Trusted researcher identity |
| Data access and authorisation | Request and authorise access |
| Governance, policy and operations | Govern & sustain |

Output type describes what the catalogue item is: the thing a user can find, request, use, read, attend, or refer to.
Use the following **output types**<a id="output-types"></a> consistently across the site:

- Service _(A user-facing capability, workflow, platform or support offering that someone can access, request or use)_
- Software / tool _(Code, application, script, package, workflow or repository)_
- Data resource _(Dataset, data commons, repository collection, reference resource or data asset)_
- Infrastructure component _(Reusable technical infrastructure, deployment pattern, integration, environment or platform component)_
- Documentation / guidance _(Written guidance, policy notes, procedures, standards guidance, implementation guides)_
- Publication / report _(Formal publication, white paper, report, preprint, poster or public presentation output)_

Use the following service/resource **status values**<a id="status-values"></a> consistently across the site:

- Available _(accessible or usable now, subject to any stated access conditions)_
- Pilot _(active but limited to specific users, cohorts or partners; still being evaluated)_
- In development _(being built, tested or documented)_

Use the following **visibility/access values**<a id="visibility-access-values"></a> consistently across the site:

- Public _(openly visible and accessible, or publicly documented)_
- Controlled access _(available only to eligible users, cohorts, partners or controlled-access workflows)_
- Internal _(not directly user-facing)_

Contribution type describes the kind of work, expertise, or role contributed by a partner. A single catalogue item or partner entry may have multiple contribution types.
Use the following **contribution types**<a id="contribution-types"></a> consistently across the site:

- Technical delivery _(Designing, building, integrating or maintaining software, workflows, platforms or technical components)_
- Infrastructure operations _(Hosting, compute, storage, deployment, security operations or operational support)_
- Data stewardship _(Data curation, metadata, repository management, dataset preparation, data governance implementation)_
- Governance / expertise _(Governance, ethical, legal, social, policy, access governance, consent, risk or compliance expertise)_
- Standards / interoperability _(Alignment with standards, schemas, APIs, interoperability models or global infrastructure patterns)_
- Training / capability building _(Developing or delivering training, onboarding, documentation, workforce development or user support capability)_
- Community engagement _(Workshops, consultation, user testing, partner engagement, community feedback and adoption work)_
- Program coordination _(Coordination, project delivery, cross-partner alignment, reporting, program governance or synthesis work)_

Use the central organisation vocabulary in `src/data/vocabularies.ts` for catalogue lead and contributing organisation values. Catalogue metadata should use canonical organisation names; display contexts may use the associated short names where space is limited. Examples:

- National Computational Infrastructure → NCI
- Australian BioCommons → BioCommons
- Australian Access Federation → AAF
- University of Sydney → USyd
- QIMR Berghofer Medical Research Institute → QIMR Berghofer

Use the central intended-user vocabulary in `src/data/vocabularies.ts` for catalogue audience chips. Add new audience values deliberately rather than inventing near-duplicates in individual catalogue records.

Use `projectSlug` values on partner contribution entries as the source of truth for catalogue/project linkage.

- Each partner contribution entry must define a unique `projectSlug`.
- Catalogue `partnerContributions` values must reference existing partner contribution `projectSlug` values.

## Partner representation

Partners should be visible in three places:

1. homepage partner section;
2. partner attribution on catalogue items;
3. structured partner contribution index.

The site should avoid making institutional structure the primary navigation model.

## Content hierarchy

Prioritise:

1. what GUARDIANS is;
2. what users can use or learn from;
3. what capabilities are being built;
4. who contributed;
5. program background and governance.

Do not lead with governance, internal work packages or partner-by-partner project descriptions.

## Claim discipline

- Do not imply that an output is available if it is a pilot, in-development item or internal/enabling contribution.
- Do not imply that every contribution is a public-facing service.
- Do not describe partner contributions unevenly through long prose for some partners and short entries for others.
- Use status and visibility/access labels to make maturity and availability clear.

# Decisions

## Decisions made

- Build and maintain five top-level pages: Home, Services & resources, Partners & contributions, About, Contact.
- Use capability-first navigation, not project-first or partner-first navigation.
- Treat the Services & resources catalogue as the core reusable content model.
- Use explicit project-to-catalogue linkage via controlled partner contribution slugs rather than deriving relationships only from organisation attribution.
- Partner organisations are:
  - Australian Access Federation (AAF)
  - National Computational Infrastructure (NCI)
  - University of Sydney — ELSI project
  - University of Melbourne — ELSI, OMIX3/PX4, CCGCM projects
  - Children's Cancer Institute / Zero Childhood Cancer Program (CCI/ZERO)
  - QIMR Berghofer Medical Research Institute
  - Garvan Medical Research Institute
- Represent partners through attribution, contribution types and a partner contribution index.
- Include outputs that are not public-facing services, such as code, documentation, guidance, infrastructure components, training, and expertise.
- Name page content data files by the user-facing page name, even when Astro route files use routing conventions. For example, `src/pages/index.astro` renders Home page content from `src/data/home.ts`.

## Open decisions

- Final catalogue entries and descriptions for the public catalogue.
- Long-term contact destination: shared email, form, or BioCommons contact route.
- Whether catalogue cards should eventually move from a disclosure-based metadata pattern to dedicated item detail pages.

---

# Site success criteria

The site is successful if:

- a new visitor can understand GUARDIANS within 30 seconds;
- a researcher can find a relevant service or resource within two clicks;
- a partner can see how their contribution is represented;
- funders/media can see credible national coordination and delivery;
- the site has no placeholder text, unclear quotes or misleading claims;
- all catalogue items have consistent metadata;
- this structure can support future growth without redesign.
