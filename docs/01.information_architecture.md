# GUARDIANS public web presence — one-page information architecture

## Audience for this document

BioCommons internal stakeholders, content strategists, project managers, designers and developers.

## Purpose

Build a public web presence for GUARDIANS that explains the program, helps users discover services and resources, and represents partner contributions clearly and fairly.

The site should support two linked modes:

1. **Program communication** — what GUARDIANS is, why it exists, who coordinates, funds and delivers it.
2. **Resource discovery** — what services, tools, guidance, infrastructure, code, training and pilots have been produced or are in development.

Primary organising principle:

> Users navigate by capability and need. Partners are represented through attribution, contribution trails and a dedicated contribution index.

---

## Primary audiences

| Audience                   | Main question                                   | Primary destination                     |
| -------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Funders, government, media | What is GUARDIANS and is it delivering?         | Home, About                             |
| Researcher users           | What can I use or learn from?                   | Services & resources                    |
| Existing partners          | Is our work represented accurately?             | Partners & contributions                |
| Potential partners         | Where could we contribute or collaborate?       | Home, Partners & contributions, Contact |

# Site map and page requirements

## 1. `/` — Home

### Page purpose

Orient visitors quickly, establish credibility, and direct them to the most relevant next step.

### Key user questions

- What is GUARDIANS?
- Why does it matter?
- What is being built?
- Who is involved?
- Where should I go next?

### Required sections

1. **Hero**
   - One-sentence definition.
   - Short supporting paragraph.
   - Primary CTA: **Explore services & resources**
   - Secondary CTA: **See partner contributions**

   Suggested message:

   > GUARDIANS is a BioCommons-coordinated national infrastructure program, delivered with partners, supporting secure, responsible discovery, access, sharing and analysis of sensitive human omics data.

2. **Why GUARDIANS exists**
   - Plain-language problem statement.
   - Focus on fragmentation, sensitivity of human omics data, access barriers, governance complexity and the need for trusted national infrastructure.

3. **What GUARDIANS is building**
   - Capability cards linking to catalogue filters or grouped catalogue sections.
   - Use the controlled capability vocabulary:
     - Data discovery
     - Secure access
     - Data commons and repositories
     - Federated and scalable analysis
     - Governance, policy and operations
     - Training and community capability

4. **Featured services & resources**
   - 3–6 high-value or representative catalogue items.
   - Each card should show name, type, status, capability area and lead partner.
   - Link each featured item to the relevant catalogue item, filtered view or grouped catalogue section.

5. **Delivered with partners**
   - Short explanation of the delivery model.
   - Partner logo/list strip.
   - Link to `/partners-contributions`.
   - Keep this as a credibility and orientation section; do not duplicate the full partner contribution index.

6. **Evidence of activity**
   - Short list of recent outputs, updates, releases, events, publications or repositories.
   - Keep this lightweight for the first build.

7. **Contact / next step**
   - Short CTA block linking to `/contact`.

### Build notes

- Home should act as a routing page, not a complete explanation of everything.
- Include a link to `/about` from either **Why GUARDIANS exists** or **Delivered with partners**.
- Reuse the canonical one-sentence description consistently.

## 2. `/services-resources` — Services & resources catalogue

### Page purpose

Help users discover GUARDIANS outputs by capability, type, status, visibility and partner.

This is the main utility page.

### Key user questions

- What has GUARDIANS produced?
- What can I use now?
- What is in development?
- Which capability area does this relate to?
- Who led or contributed to it?
- Where do I go next?

### Required sections

1. **Catalogue introduction**
   - Explain that the catalogue includes available services as well as enabling resources, pilots, infrastructure components, guidance, code, training and publications.
   - Avoid implying that every item is a public-facing service.

2. **Filters or grouped sections**
   - Capability area
   - Output type
   - Status
   - Visibility / access
   - Partner

   For v0.1, these may be functional filters or static grouped sections.

3. **Catalogue cards**

   | Field                 |          Required | Notes                                                                                              |
   | --------------------- | ----------------: | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
   | Name                  |               Yes | Clear title                                                                                        |
   | Short description     |               Yes | One or two sentences                                                                               |
   | User value            |               Yes | What someone can do with it                                                                        |
   | Capability area       |               Yes | One primary capability                                                                             |
   | Output type           |               Yes | Service, tool, infrastructure component, guidance, training, code, publication, pilot              |
   | Status                |               Yes | Available, pilot, in development, planned                                                          |
   | Visibility / access   |               Yes | Public, limited access, internal / enabling                                                        |
   | Lead partner          |               Yes | Primary attribution                                                                                |
   | Contributing partners |          Optional | Shared attribution                                                                                 |
   | Contribution type     |               Yes | Public service, technical component, governance/policy, training, infrastructure, pilot, expertise |
   | Link                  | Yes, if available | External resource, repository, publication, service page or contact pathway                        |
   | Intended users        |          Optional | Researchers, data custodians, infrastructure providers, partners                                   |

4. **Status guidance**

   Use status for maturity/readiness:

   - **Available** — accessible or usable now, subject to any stated access conditions.
   - **Pilot** — active but limited to specific users, cohorts or partners; still being evaluated.
   - **In development** — being built, tested or documented.
   - **Planned** — scoped or intended, but not yet available.

5. **Visibility / access guidance**

   Use visibility/access for who can see or use an item:

   - **Public** — openly visible and accessible, or publicly documented.
   - **Limited access** — available only to eligible users, cohorts, partners or controlled-access workflows.
   - **Internal / enabling** — not directly user-facing, but supports delivery of GUARDIANS infrastructure or partner outputs.

6. **Catalogue CTA**
   - “Can’t find what you need? Contact the GUARDIANS team.”

### Build notes

- Catalogue cards should link to partner entries where useful.
- If an item has no public destination, link to a contact pathway or partner contribution entry rather than leaving it feeling unfinished.
- Do not treat “internal / enabling” as a status. It is a visibility/access classification.

## 3. `/partners-contributions` — Partner contribution index

### Page purpose

Represent partner work fairly while preserving user-oriented navigation elsewhere.

This page should show how partner contributions map to GUARDIANS capabilities and outputs. It should not become a set of institutional marketing pages.

### Key user questions

- Who is involved in GUARDIANS?
- What contribution areas is each partner involved in?
- Where does each partner’s work appear in the catalogue?
- How does partner delivery fit into the overall program?

### Required sections

1. **Partner delivery model**
   - Explain that GUARDIANS is coordinated by Australian BioCommons and delivered with national partners.
   - Clarify that contributions include public-facing services and enabling work.

2. **Contribution types**

   Use this controlled contribution type vocabulary:

   - Public service
   - Technical component
   - Infrastructure deployment
   - Governance / policy
   - Standards / interoperability
   - Training / documentation
   - Pilot / exemplar
   - Specialist expertise
   - Operational support

3. **Partner index**

   Each partner entry should include:

   | Field                      | Required | Notes                      |
   | -------------------------- | -------: | -------------------------- |
   | Partner name               |      Yes | Organisation name          |
   | Short role statement       |      Yes | One sentence               |
   | Contribution areas         |      Yes | Mapped to capability areas |
   | Contribution types         |      Yes | From taxonomy above        |
   | Related services/resources |      Yes | Links into catalogue       |
   | External link              | Optional | Partner or project page    |

4. **Cross-links**
   - Each partner should link to related catalogue items or filtered catalogue views.
   - Catalogue items should link back to partner contribution entries where useful.

### Build notes

- Avoid long partner narrative profiles in the first build.
- Use structured entries so representation is consistent and maintainable.
- Do not make the site primarily partner-first; this page exists to support attribution and findability.

## 4. `/about` — Program background, funding and coordination

### Page purpose

Provide authoritative context for the program without overloading the homepage.

### Key user questions

- What is GUARDIANS?
- Who coordinates it?
- How is it funded?
- What phase is it in?
- How does it relate to national digital research infrastructure?

### Required sections

1. **Program overview**
   - What GUARDIANS is.
   - What problem it addresses.
   - Who it is intended to support.

2. **Program timeline**
   - Total program length: 3.5 years.
   - Phase I: 2 years.
   - Phase II: 18 months.
   - Current state: 18 months into Phase I
   - (link to or embed project tracker)

3. **Coordination and delivery**
   - Australian BioCommons coordination role.
   - Partner delivery model.
   - Relationship to the broader national infrastructure ecosystem.

4. **Funding acknowledgement**
   - Recognise BPA and NCRIS funding/support as appropriate.
   - Use approved wording when available.

5. **Program scope**
   - What GUARDIANS is building.
   - What the site/catalogue covers.
   - What may still be in development.

6. **Related links**
   - BioCommons GUARDIANS page.
   - Program announcements.
   - Relevant partner or funder pages.

7. **Next step**
   - Link to `/services-resources` as the concrete expression of program delivery.

### Build notes

- This page should be credible and factual, not promotional.
- Avoid duplicating the homepage. Home orients; About explains.
- About should not be a dead end: link users back to the catalogue and contact pathways.

---

## 5. `/contact` — Contact pathways

### Page purpose

Give different audiences a clear next step without requiring them to understand the program structure first.

### Key user questions

- Who do I contact?
- How do I ask about using something?
- How do I discuss collaboration?
- How do I provide feedback?

### Required sections

1. **Contact intro**
   - Short statement inviting enquiries.

2. **Audience-based pathways**

   Use 3–4 contact cards:

   - **Use a service or resource**
     - For researchers, data custodians or infrastructure users.
     - Secondary link: `/services-resources`

   - **Discuss collaboration**
     - For potential partners or peer programs.
     - Secondary link: `/partners-contributions`

   - **Media or funder enquiry**
     - For communications, reporting or program-level questions.
     - Secondary link: `/about`

   - **Give feedback on this site**
     - For prototype/user-testing feedback.

3. **Contact mechanism**
   - Email address, form, or BioCommons contact route.
   - For the first build, one shared contact route is acceptable if pathways are labelled clearly.

4. **Response expectation**
   - Optional short line about the team reviewing enquiries.

### Build notes

- Contact should not be the only next step. Where possible, help users self-serve by linking back to relevant pages.
- Keep contact pathways audience-based, not organisationally structured.

# Cross-linking rules

- All pages are available through global navigation.
- Home capability cards link to relevant catalogue filters or grouped sections.
- Home featured services/resources link to catalogue items, filtered views or grouped sections.
- Home partner section links to `/partners-contributions`.
- Home program-background links point to `/about`.
- Catalogue cards link to related partner entries where available.
- Partner entries link to related catalogue items, filtered catalogue views or grouped sections.
- About links to `/services-resources` as the concrete expression of program delivery.
- Contact cards link back to relevant self-service pages where useful.
- External links should not replace internal structure; use them as next-step destinations once the user understands the GUARDIANS context.

# Cross-site content rules

## Language

Use plain, direct language.

Prefer:

> omics

Avoid:

> genomics and omics

Use:

> services and resources

rather than only:

> services

because not all outputs are public-facing services.

## Controlled vocabulary

Use the following capability areas consistently across the site:

- Data discovery
- Secure access
- Data commons and repositories
- Federated and scalable analysis
- Governance, policy and operations
- Training and community capability

Use the following contribution types consistently across the site:

- Public service
- Technical component
- Infrastructure deployment
- Governance / policy
- Standards / interoperability
- Training / documentation
- Pilot / exemplar
- Specialist expertise
- Operational support

Use the following status values consistently across the site:

- Available
- Pilot
- In development
- Planned

Use the following visibility/access values consistently across the site:

- Public
- Limited access
- Internal / enabling

## Partner representation

Partners should be visible in three places:

1. homepage partner section;
2. partner attribution on catalogue items;
3. structured partner contribution index.

The site should avoid making institutional structure the primary navigation model.

## Content hierarchy

Prioritise:

1. what GUARDIANS is;
2. what users can use or learn from;
3. what capabilities are being built;
4. who contributed;
5. program background and governance.

Do not lead with governance, internal work packages or partner-by-partner project descriptions.

## Claim discipline

- Do not imply that an output is available if it is a pilot, planned item or internal/enabling contribution.
- Do not imply that every contribution is a public-facing service.
- Do not describe partner contributions unevenly through long prose for some partners and short entries for others.
- Use status and visibility/access labels to make maturity and availability clear.

# Decisions

## Decisions made

- Build five pages in the first prototype: Home, Services & resources, Partners & contributions, About, Contact.
- Use capability-first navigation, not project-first or partner-first navigation.
- Treat the Services & resources catalogue as the core reusable content model.
- Represent partners through attribution, contribution types and a partner contribution index.
- Use “services and resources” rather than “services” alone.
- Use “omics” rather than “genomics”.
- Include outputs that are not public-facing services, such as guidance, infrastructure components, code, training, pilots and expertise.
- Separate **status** from **visibility/access** in the catalogue model.

## Open decisions

- Final list of partner organisations.
- Final catalogue entries for the first prototype.
- Approved funding acknowledgement wording.
- Contact destination: shared email, form, or BioCommons contact route.
- Whether catalogue filtering is functional in v0.1 or presented as grouped sections.

---

# Initial build success criteria

The first build is successful if:

- a new visitor can understand GUARDIANS within 30 seconds;
- a researcher can find a relevant service or resource within two clicks;
- a partner can see how their contribution is represented;
- funders/media can see credible national coordination and delivery;
- the site has no placeholder text, unclear quotes or misleading claims;
- all catalogue items have consistent metadata;
- the structure can support future growth without redesign.
